**syntax-based questions** to **thinking-skill categories**

## 1. Sequence Problems

(Instructions executed step by step in order)

1. Read three numbers and print their sum.
2. Convert hours into minutes.
3. Find area of a triangle.
4. Calculate simple interest.
5. Convert Celsius to Fahrenheit.
6. Find average marks of 5 subjects.
7. Convert total seconds into hours, minutes, seconds.
8. Compute total bill with tax.
9. Find perimeter of a rectangle.
10. Calculate final price after discount.

3. Find largest among three numbers.
4. Check leap year.
5. Find grade based on marks.
6. Check whether a number is divisible by both 3 and 5.
7. Determine if a person can vote.
8. Find profit or loss.
9. Check triangle validity.
10. Find whether a character is vowel or consonant.

6. Count digits in a number.

8. Find sum of digits.

7. Find repeated element in a list.
8. Print Floyd’s triangle.
9. Identify odd one out in a number pattern.
10. Predict next term in Fibonacci pattern.

## 7. Greedy Problems (Basic)

(Make best choice at each step)

1. Find minimum coins for an amount.
2. Make change using notes 500,100,50,10.
3. Select largest numbers to reach target sum.
4. Fill bag with highest-value items first.
5. Schedule maximum activities without overlap.
6. Minimize number of jumps to reach end (basic version).
7. Choose smallest number of books to reach page target.
8. Find shortest path choosing nearest next step (simple).
9. Pack items until bag is full.
10. Select students with highest marks first until quota filled.

## 8. Brute Force Problems

5. Find palindrome by testing all substrings.
6. Check all combinations of three numbers summing to target.
7. Find all permutations of a word.
8. Find maximum sum subarray by checking every subarray.
9. Check all routes in a simple maze.
10. Try every password combination (small size).

# Cross-Category Challenge Questions (Mixed Thinking)

These combine multiple skills:

1. Prime numbers in a range (Selection + Iteration)
2. Two Sum (Brute Force + Algorithmic)
3. Coin Change (Greedy + Decomposition)
4. Fibonacci Pattern (Iteration + Pattern Recognition)
5. ATM Simulator (Simulation + Decomposition)
6. Student Grading System (Sequence + Selection)
7. Activity Selection (Greedy + Algorithmic)
8. Sorting without built-ins (Iteration + Algorithmic)
9. Palindrome Checker (Pattern + Decomposition)
10. Maze Traversal (Simulation + Brute Force)

---

## These categories map roughly to computer science concepts:

* Sequence → Procedural thinking
* Selection → Logic/Decision making
* Iteration → Repetition
* Pattern Recognition → Computational thinking
* Decomposition → Problem breakdown
* Simulation → Modeling
* Greedy → Optimization
* Brute Force → Exhaustive search
* Algorithmic Thinking → Algorithm design
